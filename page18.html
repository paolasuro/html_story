The Yellow House

Authors Bio's, About

You begin to climb up the stairs being cautious just like before. You jump at every creaking sound the stairs make, keeping your fingers crossed that none of the steps break. You make it upstairs and there are only two doors. The one on your left leads to a bathroom. Instead of a matching pastel pink bathroom like the kitchen, it was a pastel green. There was no wallpaper but it had tile covering the walls and floor. You search the bathroom and find nothing. You try the other door and it leads to a bedroom. The room is small. It has blue and green floral wallpaper. The bed is up against the corner to the left with a tiny wooden nightstand next to it and a dresser on the left side of the wall. The bedding is a faded blue and has holes it in from the moths over the years. You search through the dresser but come up empty-handed, just old clothes that were left behind. You don't give up and look through the nightstand. And in the last drawer there is a faded drawing of what looks like the layout of the house with an X on some part of the backyard.

Give up and go home. Go to the back yard. If you have the journal, old pictures, and a key you can go back to the pantry and open the small door.
